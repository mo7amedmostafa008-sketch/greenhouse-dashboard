The Challenge

Engineering the Living System

Issued by the Central Unit for Gifted and Talented Students — Egypt — 2025–2026.

Capstone Big Idea

Design and implement an automated closed-loop control system to monitor and regulate essential environmental variables in aquaculture, greenhouse agriculture, or hybrid aquaponics — triggering corrective actions from real-time sensor feedback. The system must be constructed using waste or recycled materials wherever possible.

Essential Question

How can automated feedback systems maintain environmental conditions to support agricultural and fish production across diverse regional farming contexts in Egypt?

Egypt Grand Challenges Addressed

  • Increase the industrial and agricultural base of Egypt
  • Recycle garbage and waste for economic and environmental purposes
  • Address consequences of rapid population growth
  • Reduce pollution affecting air, water, and soil

Key Design Requirements

  • Monitor ≥ 3 environmental parameters via calibrated sensors
  • Implement ≥ 2 actuators responding to sensor feedback
  • Operate autonomously for ≥ 6 hours without manual intervention
  • Built from recycled / waste materials
  • Log data over 5 consecutive outdoor days
Our Solution

Smart Greenhouse Prototype

A fully automated, closed-loop system built on Arduino Uno, enclosed in a recycled wooden dome and planted with strawberries and chives.

Sensors

🌡️

DS18B20 Temperature Sensor

Waterproof digital probe, ±0.5°C accuracy; triggers fan and ventilation on high readings

💧

Capacitive Soil Moisture Sensor

Calibrated with hysteresis control to prevent pump oscillation

☀️

LDR Light Sensor

Monitors ambient light intensity; modulates ventilation rate accordingly

Actuators & Structure

💦

Water Pump (Irrigation)

Activates when soil moisture falls below the 55% threshold setpoint

🌀

Cooling Fan

Convection-based cooling embedded opposite the dome window; activates above 28°C

🔧

Stepper Motor — Window Control

Opens / closes the ventilation window based on temperature and light conditions

🌿

Recycled Dome Structure

Semi-spherical wooden dome over a plastic storage container filled with soil; fully portable

System Architecture

Closed-Loop Feedback Control

The system continuously measures sensor readings, compares them to target setpoints, and actuates corrective responses — no manual input required.

Scientific Analysis

Why the System Works

The prototype operates as a negative feedback control system: each sensor continuously measures its respective parameter and returns that data to the Arduino controller, which computes the error between the measured value and the desired setpoint. When deviation is detected, the appropriate actuator is triggered to restore equilibrium.

Hysteresis bands prevent rapid oscillatory switching — the pump, for example, does not toggle on and off repeatedly around the 55% boundary, but instead activates below 55% and deactivates only after moisture reaches 65%.

The wooden semi-spherical dome creates a controlled microclimate, reducing thermal loss and concentrating humidity — improving the efficiency of both the temperature regulation and the irrigation subsystem. The system successfully stabilized plant growth conditions, demonstrating that closed-loop automation built from recycled materials can provide a practical and cost-effective approach to precision agriculture — directly addressing Egypt's food security and sustainability challenges.
